# Juha Jattu - Digital Design - Development Aid - Industrial Design ## Digital Design ### My Process I studied industrial design in Lahti Polytechnic, Institute of Design, at a campus where we shared a lunch cafeteria with students of other occupations. A few years into our studies, we made note of how easily we could distinguish the typical students at the lunch restaurant just by looking at the habitus: the engineer students with their jeans, shirts and teenage mustaches, the trade students with their tidy business coats, and us design students who might appear to a coffee break directly out of our metal of wood workshops in dirty overalls, tools hanging by our waist. That early experience gave birth to my idea of design process in general, that is largely similar to both industrial design of physical objects and digital design of services and user-interfaces. 1. Question The goal of any design work is to create a change in something, hopefully for the better, and it all starts with a question raised about the current state of affairs in some domain. What if we created a new product? What if we acquired a new CMS? The questions proposed initially may however not be the most relevant questions.
